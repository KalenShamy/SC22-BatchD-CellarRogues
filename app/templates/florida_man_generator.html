Our purpose


The story of Florida Man...



Since Florida is undoubtedly the most ridiculous and nonsensical state in the entire US, this project is designed to reveal the weirdest activities and antics of the locals! We are also testing how similar AI generated news headlines can sound to real ones.

Numerous Florida citizens have done something so crazy that it seems like it was out of a wild dream! In a group of headlines from different states, you can trust that Florida Man will always take the cake. There are tons of crazy headlines generated by the GPT-2 model in this website from the data in the Florida Man Dataset that are sure to blow your mind.

Without further ado, let's take a look at some shenanigans from Florida Man!

PROCESS

Step 1

Collect Data

We used Kaggle to look for different Florida Man data sets to use. After a bit of searching, we found a data set that consists of different posts from the subreddit, r/FloridaMan.

Florida Man Data Set

Pre-Processing

As we were looking through our dataset, we found multiple spam messages and unrelated posts in our data set. We used the python library, pandas, and different python methods to remove all of the unrelated posts from the dataset.

Step 2
Step 3

Train Model

We used Aitextgen to fine-tune an existing GPT-2 model and then trained the model for about 20 minutes on our filtered data set.

Post-Processing

To make our project more believable we generated mugshots to go with our headlines. To do this we compiled a file of different male and female mugshots. We had the model randomly pick a mugshot depending if the the AI generated a headline to do with Florida Man or Woman. We also used the package, Better_Profanity, to ensure that our headlines were clean. Lastly to we capitalized the main words in our headline.

Step 4
Step 5

Deploy Product

After creating a working model, we used Flask to deploy it.


Challenges and Solutions

Issue: Spam

As we examined our dataset, we found different spam and off-topic posts.

Solution:

We used a couple python methods to filter out all the headlines which didn't contain the keyword florida and then we filtered out all of the ones that had over 20 words.

Issue: Credibility

As we examined the headlines the model produced, we realized that they didn't sound realistic.

Solution:

We increased the amount of time the machine trained, added capitalization and mugshots.

Issue: Language

As we examined the headlines the model produced, we realized that some of them used curse words.

Solution:

We then used one of the filtering methods from the package, better_profanity to bleep out the inappropriate language.
